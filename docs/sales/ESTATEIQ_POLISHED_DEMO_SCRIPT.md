# EstateIQ Polished Demo Script

## Purpose

This script is designed for a 10-minute EstateIQ demo with luxury property managers, home watch companies, boutique estate operators, and concierge property teams.

The goal is not to show every feature. The goal is to tell a clear story:

> EstateIQ turns every property visit into a polished, documented, homeowner-ready record.

## Demo Mindset

Keep the demo calm, confident, and simple.

Do not rush through every tab. Do not apologize for the product being early. Do not over-explain technical details.

The buyer should walk away thinking:

- This would make us look more professional.
- This would make inspections more consistent.
- This would help homeowners trust our service.
- This would reduce the mess of texts, photos, notes, and reports.

## Best Demo Setup

Use a polished property with:

- a strong exterior property photo
- homeowner name
- address
- inspection checklist
- inspection photos
- at least one issue or note
- web report
- PDF report

Recommended demo path:

1. Landing page
2. Home
3. Property
4. Inspect
5. Issues
6. Reports
7. Web report
8. PDF report
9. Owner view, if time allows

## Opening: 30 Seconds

Say:

> EstateIQ is built for luxury property oversight: home watch, inspections, owner updates, issue tracking, and homeowner-ready reports.
>
> The problem we are solving is that a lot of high-end property care still happens through texts, scattered photos, spreadsheets, email threads, and memory.
>
> EstateIQ gives the operator a calmer system of record and gives the homeowner a more polished, trustworthy service experience.

Transition:

> I’ll walk you through the core workflow from selecting a property to generating the homeowner report.

## Step 1: Landing Page

Time: 30 seconds

Show:

- EstateIQ landing page
- luxury positioning
- clear role entry

Say:

> The brand is intentionally premium and calm. This is not meant to feel like generic property management software. EstateIQ is built to feel closer to a digital estate manager: organized, discreet, and homeowner-friendly.

Do not spend too long here. Move quickly into the app.

Transition:

> Now I’ll step into the operator workflow.

## Step 2: Home

Time: 1 minute

Show:

- Today’s properties
- property cards
- property photos
- View Property
- Inspect
- Add New Property

Say:

> Home is where the operator starts the day. Each property has a reference photo so the inspector can immediately confirm the correct home.
>
> From here, the workflow is intentionally simple: view the property or begin the inspection.

Point out:

- The property photo helps prevent confusion.
- The inspector does not need to dig through a complicated dashboard.
- The main action is clear.

Value line:

> For field teams, clarity matters. The goal is fewer taps, fewer missed details, and less confusion about which property is being inspected.

Transition:

> Before starting the inspection, I’ll open the property profile.

## Step 3: Property Profile

Time: 1 minute

Show:

- property image
- owner
- address
- phone/email
- access notes
- vendors/property contacts
- edit property button

Say:

> The property profile keeps the important information tied to the home: owner details, address, contact information, access notes, vendor contacts, and the property reference photo.
>
> This is helpful when more than one person touches the account. Everyone is working from the same property record.

Value line:

> In luxury property management, details matter. EstateIQ helps make those details easier to find and harder to lose.

Transition:

> Now I’ll move into the actual inspection.

## Step 4: Inspection

Time: 2 minutes

Show:

- selected property reference
- inspection type
- inspector name
- interior temperature
- checklist
- notes
- photos
- urgent flag
- Generate Report

Say:

> The inspection flow is designed around the field visit. The inspector selects the inspection type, confirms the visit details, completes the checklist, adds notes and photos, and then generates the report.
>
> The checklist creates consistency. It helps make sure every property visit follows the same standard, even when different team members are doing the work.

If showing photos:

> Photos are attached directly to the inspection instead of living separately in a phone gallery, text thread, or shared folder.

If showing notes:

> Notes and issues become part of the record, so the office does not have to reconstruct what happened later.

Value line:

> EstateIQ turns field work into structured business information.

Transition:

> If something needs attention, it can be handled as an issue.

## Step 5: Issues

Time: 1 minute

Show:

- open issue list
- add issue
- priority
- status
- vendor
- next step
- issue photos if available

Say:

> Issues are where maintenance concerns or follow-up items are documented. The important thing is that the issue is tied back to the property and the inspection context.
>
> Instead of the team searching through texts or remembering who said what, the issue has a clear status, priority, vendor, and next step.

Value line:

> This helps prevent small issues from getting buried and helps the operator show homeowners that follow-up is being managed.

Transition:

> The most important homeowner-facing piece is the report.

## Step 6: Reports

Time: 1 minute

Show:

- Reports tab
- current report
- Open Web Report
- Download PDF File

Say:

> This is where EstateIQ turns the inspection into a homeowner-ready record.
>
> The operator can open the report in the browser or download a polished PDF.

Value line:

> This is one of the biggest differences from ordinary workflows. The homeowner is not just getting a few texts and photos. They are getting a professional record of the visit.

Transition:

> I’ll open the web report first.

## Step 7: Web Report

Time: 1 minute

Show:

- property-first hero image
- property status
- concise details
- concierge notes
- inspection checks
- issues/notes
- photo documentation

Say:

> The web report is designed to feel like a luxury homeowner summary, not a generic software export.
>
> The property image leads the report because the first thing the homeowner wants to know is: this is my home, and it was reviewed.
>
> Then the report gives them the important information: status, visit details, notes, completed checks, and photo documentation.

Value line:

> This builds trust because it makes the service visible.

Transition:

> For homeowners who prefer a file, there is also a PDF version.

## Step 8: PDF Report

Time: 1 minute

Show:

- PDF cover
- summary/details page
- photo gallery page

Say:

> The PDF is meant to feel like a premium owner packet. It is concise, branded, and easy to review.
>
> It gives the operator something polished to send, save, or attach to an owner update.

Point out:

- property photo cover
- luxury styling
- concise page count
- grouped photo documentation

Value line:

> A polished report helps the operator justify premium service pricing and makes the homeowner feel cared for.

Transition:

> If time allows, I’ll show the owner view.

## Optional Step 9: Owner View

Time: 1 minute

Show:

- owner-facing status
- latest report
- simple updates

Say:

> The owner view is intentionally simple. Homeowners should not see operational clutter.
>
> They should see the property status, the latest report, and the most relevant updates.

Value line:

> The owner experience should feel calm, private, and reassuring.

## Closing: 1 Minute

Say:

> That is the core EstateIQ workflow: property profile, inspection, issue documentation, report generation, and homeowner-ready presentation.
>
> The product is designed to help operators do three things:
>
> 1. Document property visits more consistently.
> 2. Communicate with homeowners more professionally.
> 3. Create a premium service experience that supports trust and retention.

Then ask:

> In your current workflow, where does this feel most useful: inspections, photos, issue tracking, or homeowner reports?

## Questions To Ask After The Demo

Use these to turn the demo into a sales conversation:

1. How are you currently sending homeowners inspection updates?
2. How long does it take your team to prepare a report after a visit?
3. Where do photos usually live today?
4. What happens when an issue is found during a visit?
5. Do homeowners ever ask for more proof or more detail?
6. Would a report like this help you justify a premium service tier?
7. Who on your team would use this first?
8. What would need to be true for you to pilot this on 3-5 properties?

## Short Version: 5-Minute Demo

Use this if the prospect is short on time.

1. Home:
   > This is where the operator starts the day and selects the property.

2. Property:
   > This keeps the key property details, photo, owner information, and vendor contacts in one place.

3. Inspect:
   > The inspector completes a guided visit, adds photos and notes, and generates the report.

4. Web Report:
   > The homeowner gets a clean property-first report instead of scattered texts and photos.

5. PDF:
   > The operator can also send a branded PDF owner packet.

Close:

> EstateIQ helps luxury property teams make every visit visible, documented, and homeowner-ready.

## What Not To Say

Avoid:

- "This is like a task manager."
- "This replaces all your software."
- "The AI does everything."
- "This is just an inspection checklist."
- "It is still rough."
- "We can build anything you want."

Better language:

- "EstateIQ is focused on property oversight and homeowner trust."
- "The pilot starts small so we can validate the real workflow."
- "The product is designed to support the operator, not replace judgment."
- "The report is one of the key value drivers."

## Best Closing Line

> EstateIQ is not just about documenting the visit. It is about making the homeowner feel that their property is being watched with care, consistency, and professionalism.
