# EstateIQ Prospecting Strategy

Goal: secure the first 5 EstateIQ pilot customers.

Primary target:

```text
Home watch companies and boutique luxury property operators with 5-50 premium homes.
```

The first 5 pilot customers should help prove:

- operators want a better property visit workflow
- reports create clear value
- photos and issues need a central record
- homeowners respond positively to polished updates
- customers are willing to pay

## Prospecting Positioning

EstateIQ should not be positioned as generic SaaS.

Position EstateIQ as:

```text
A luxury home watch and property oversight platform that helps operators document visits, organize photos, track issues, and deliver homeowner-ready reports.
```

Simple one-liner:

```text
EstateIQ turns every property visit into a polished homeowner-ready record.
```

Founder-led message:

```text
I am building EstateIQ for luxury home watch and property care companies that want a more professional way to document visits, organize photos, track issues, and communicate with homeowners.
```

## Email Strategy

### Email Cadence

Use a simple 4-touch email sequence.

Recommended timing:

```text
Day 1: Initial email
Day 4: Follow-up
Day 9: Value/example email
Day 16: Breakup email
```

### Email 1: Initial Outreach

Subject options:

- Quick question about your home watch reports
- Improving homeowner updates after property visits
- EstateIQ pilot for home watch companies

Email:

```text
Hi [Name],

I am building EstateIQ for luxury home watch and property care teams that want a more professional way to document visits, organize photos, track issues, and send homeowner-ready reports.

I noticed [Company] works with premium homes in [Market], and I wanted to ask how you currently handle inspection reports and homeowner updates after visits.

I am looking for a small number of pilot customers to help shape the product.

Would you be open to a quick 15-minute conversation?

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### Email 2: Follow-Up

Subject:

```text
Re: home watch reports
```

Email:

```text
Hi [Name],

Just wanted to follow up.

EstateIQ is designed to help home watch and property care operators turn each visit into a clean property record: checklist, notes, photos, issues, and a polished homeowner report.

If your team is already sending updates manually by text, email, or PDF, I would love to learn how that process works today.

Would a quick call next week be reasonable?

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### Email 3: Value Email

Subject:

```text
What EstateIQ is trying to improve
```

Email:

```text
Hi [Name],

The main problem EstateIQ is trying to solve is simple:

Home watch operators do valuable work, but the proof of that work often gets scattered across texts, photos, emails, notes, and spreadsheets.

EstateIQ keeps the visit, photos, issues, vendors, and homeowner report connected in one professional workflow.

I am testing this with a few early operators and would value your perspective.

Open to a short conversation?

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### Email 4: Breakup Email

Subject:

```text
Should I close the loop?
```

Email:

```text
Hi [Name],

I do not want to keep bothering you.

I reached out because EstateIQ may be useful for home watch and property care teams that want better documentation and homeowner-ready reports.

If now is not the right time, no problem at all.

Should I close the loop for now?

Best,
[Your Name]
```

## LinkedIn Strategy

### LinkedIn Targets

Search for:

- home watch owner
- home watch services
- luxury property manager
- estate manager
- property care owner
- vacation home management
- concierge owner

Filter by:

- geography
- small company
- owner/founder title
- property/home services language

### Connection Request

```text
Hi [Name], I am building EstateIQ for luxury home watch and property care operators. I would enjoy connecting with others working around premium property oversight.
```

### After They Accept

Wait a day if possible, then send:

```text
Thanks for connecting. I am speaking with home watch and property care operators about how they document visits, photos, issues, and homeowner updates.

Would you be open to a quick 15-minute conversation? I am looking for a few early pilot customers and would value your perspective.
```

### LinkedIn Content Strategy

Post 2-3 times per week.

Post themes:

- why homeowner reports matter
- scattered photos are not a system
- luxury service needs proof of care
- home watch is trust-based
- what makes a property visit professional
- lessons from building EstateIQ
- before/after workflow ideas

Example post:

```text
Luxury home watch is not just about checking a property.

It is about giving the homeowner confidence that the home was inspected, documented, and cared for.

Texts and scattered photos can work early, but premium service needs a better record:

- property profile
- inspection checklist
- photos
- issues
- vendor follow-up
- homeowner-ready report

That is what we are building with EstateIQ.
```

## Prospect Qualification Score

Use a simple 1-5 score.

### 5: Excellent Fit

- home watch/luxury property care
- 5-50 premium properties
- owner/operator decision maker
- reports/photos are painful
- open to pilot

### 4: Strong Fit

- boutique property manager or concierge operator
- clear owner communication need
- may require more setup

### 3: Possible Fit

- vacation rental operator or broader property service company
- needs more qualification

### 2: Weak Fit

- too small
- unclear need
- no regular inspections
- price-sensitive

### 1: Not A Fit

- needs unrelated software
- no premium homes
- no reporting need
- not interested in feedback/pilot

## Discovery Call Conversion Goal

Target:

```text
50% of discovery calls should lead to demo.
50% of demos should lead to pilot proposal.
30-50% of proposals should become pilots.
```

Early-stage numbers will be messy. That is fine.

The goal is learning plus pilot customers.

## First Pilot Close Strategy

When a prospect is interested, keep the ask simple:

```text
Would you be open to testing EstateIQ with 3-5 properties for 30 days?
```

Then define:

- number of properties
- who will use it
- first inspection date
- feedback schedule
- pilot price
- conversion conversation date

Do not let the pilot become vague.

## Recommended First 5 Pilot Mix

Ideal mix:

```text
3 home watch companies
1 boutique luxury property manager
1 concierge/property care operator
```

Why:

- Home watch companies validate the core workflow.
- Boutique manager validates higher-value portfolio use.
- Concierge operator validates luxury service positioning.

Avoid making vacation rental operators or large firms the first majority. They may pull the roadmap too broadly too early.

## Founder Notes

The first 5 pilots are not just sales. They are product discovery.

The job is to learn:

- who feels the pain most urgently
- what language makes them respond
- what workflow they actually use
- what report format creates value
- what they will pay for
- what features are truly required

Do not chase every segment at once.

Start narrow:

```text
Luxury home watch companies in premium second-home markets.
```

Win that first.
