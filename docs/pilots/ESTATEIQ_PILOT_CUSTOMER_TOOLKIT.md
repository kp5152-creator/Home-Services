# EstateIQ Pilot Customer Toolkit

This toolkit is designed to help EstateIQ secure its first 3-5 pilot customers.

Target customers:

- luxury property managers
- home watch companies
- boutique estate operators
- concierge property service teams
- short-term rental operators with premium homes

The goal of the pilot is not to sell every possible future feature. The goal is to prove that EstateIQ makes property oversight feel more professional, more organized, and more valuable to homeowners.

## Ideal Pilot Customer Profile

Best early pilots:

- Manage 5-50 properties.
- Perform regular property visits or home watch inspections.
- Send homeowners updates, photos, texts, PDFs, or emails.
- Currently use spreadsheets, notes, texts, email, shared folders, or generic property management software.
- Care about presentation and trust.
- Have owners who expect a premium service experience.

Avoid early pilots who:

- Need a full accounting system.
- Need complex rental channel management.
- Need deep enterprise permissions on day one.
- Want a cheap commodity checklist app.
- Are not willing to give feedback.

## Discovery Call Script

Goal: understand their current workflow, pain points, owner expectations, and whether EstateIQ is a good pilot fit.

Recommended length: 20-30 minutes.

### Listen For

Strong buying signals:

- "Our reports take too long."
- "Photos are all over the place."
- "Owners want better updates."
- "We need to look more professional."
- "We use spreadsheets and texts."
- "I want something built for home watch."
- "Our current software is too much."
- "This would help us justify premium pricing."

Weak fit signals:

- "We only need accounting."
- "We just need a free checklist."
- "Our homeowners do not care about reports."
- "We do not want to change anything."
- "We need enterprise integrations before testing."

## 10-Minute Demo Script

Goal: show a simple, premium workflow. Do not over-explain. Keep it calm.

### Minute 0-1: Positioning

EstateIQ is designed for luxury property oversight. It helps operators document visits, track issues, manage vendors, schedule next steps, and deliver polished homeowner reports.

It is not generic property management software. It is built around the home watch and estate care workflow.

### Minute 1-2: Home

Start on Home.

Say:

This is where the operator sees the properties being managed. Each home has a reference photo so the inspector can quickly confirm the correct property before beginning work.

Show:

- property cards
- Add New Property
- property photo
- Inspect button
- issue notification if visible

Do not say:

- "dashboard"
- "analytics"
- "AI platform"

### Minute 2-3: Property Profile

Open a property.

Say:

This page is dedicated to the selected property. It keeps the key information in one place: owner, address, phone, email, access notes, property photo, and vendors.

Show:

- property reference photo
- homeowner contact
- access notes
- vendor contacts
- edit property

Value:

This reduces confusion in the field and gives every visit a clear property record.

### Minute 3-5: Inspection

Open Inspection.

Say:

The inspection flow is intentionally simple: choose the inspection type, confirm visit details, complete the checklist, add notes and photos, then generate the report.

Show:

- inspection type
- inspector name
- temperature
- checklist
- notes/photos
- urgent flag

Value:

The goal is fewer missed details and a consistent inspection process across the team.

### Minute 5-6: Issues

Open Issues.

Say:

If something needs attention, it becomes an issue. The operator can track priority, status, vendor, and next step.

Show:

- open issue list
- add issue
- priority/status
- vendor/next step

Value:

This keeps maintenance follow-up from getting buried in texts or emails.

### Minute 6-7: Schedule

Open Schedule.

Say:

The schedule tab is for the next visit or service appointment. It is intentionally lightweight so operators can quickly schedule the next action.

Show:

- next scheduled visit
- templates
- add visit

Value:

This connects inspections and issues to the next operational step.

### Minute 7-9: Reports

Open Reports.

Say:

This is where EstateIQ turns the inspection into a homeowner-ready record. The report can be opened in the browser or downloaded as a PDF.

Show:

- Generate Report
- report preview
- Open Web Report
- Download PDF
- visit history

Value:

This is one of the biggest differentiators: it helps the operator communicate with homeowners in a polished, professional way.

### Minute 9-10: Owner

Open Owner.

Say:

The Owner view is intentionally simple. Homeowners should not see operational clutter. They should see the status of the property, the latest report, and recent notes.

Show:

- current status
- latest report
- recent notes

Value:

This supports a luxury service experience and builds trust.

### Demo Close

The pilot would focus on three questions:

1. Does this make inspections easier?
2. Does this make owner communication more professional?
3. Does this help justify premium home watch service?

If yes, we can expand from a small pilot into a full property portfolio.

## Pilot Customer FAQ

### Is EstateIQ property management software?

EstateIQ is not traditional property management software. It is focused on luxury home watch, inspection documentation, property oversight, owner communication, reports, issues, vendors, and schedules.

### Who is EstateIQ built for?

EstateIQ is built for luxury property managers, home watch companies, concierge teams, estate operators, inspectors, and premium short-term rental operators.

### Can homeowners use EstateIQ?

Yes. The Owner view is designed to give homeowners a simple, polished view of property status, reports, and updates without operational clutter.

### Does EstateIQ replace inspectors?

No. EstateIQ supports inspectors. It helps them document visits more consistently and produce better homeowner communication.

### Does EstateIQ use AI?

EstateIQ will use AI carefully as an assistive drafting layer. AI should help create summaries and recommendations, but humans review and approve homeowner-facing content.

### Are photos stored securely?

The production plan uses private file storage. Photos should be treated as sensitive customer data and access should be controlled by user and property permissions.

### Can vendors use it?

Vendor workflows are part of the roadmap. Today, vendors can be tracked as contacts. Future versions may include vendor access and task updates.

### How long does setup take?

For a pilot, setup can be simple:

- add a few properties
- upload property photos
- enter owner/contact details
- add key vendors
- run first inspection

A small pilot can usually be set up quickly with white-glove assistance.

### What makes EstateIQ different?

EstateIQ is built for the home watch and luxury property care workflow. It is not trying to be generic task software. It focuses on trust, documentation, homeowner-ready reporting, and premium service presentation.

### What happens after the pilot?

If the pilot is successful, the customer can convert to a paid plan, expand to more properties, and help shape the next version of EstateIQ.

## Common Objection Responses

### "We already use spreadsheets."

Spreadsheets can track information, but they do not create a professional inspection workflow, photo record, report, issue history, vendor trail, and homeowner communication experience.

EstateIQ turns the visit into a polished service record.

### "We already text photos to homeowners."

Texting photos is fast, but it is not organized. Photos get buried, context gets lost, and there is no long-term property history.

EstateIQ keeps photos tied to the inspection, issue, property, and report.

### "Our current system works."

That may be true. EstateIQ is not trying to replace everything overnight.

The pilot is designed to test one focused question: can EstateIQ make inspections and homeowner communication more professional with less effort?

### "I do not want more software."

EstateIQ is being designed to reduce clutter, not add more. The workflow is simple: property, inspection, issues, schedule, reports, owner updates.

The goal is fewer scattered tools and a calmer operating system.

### "Our team is not technical."

That is exactly why EstateIQ is being built with a simple, mobile-first workflow. Inspectors should not need to be technical to use it.

### "This seems expensive."

For luxury properties, the cost of poor communication, missed issues, or unprofessional reporting can be much higher than the software cost.

EstateIQ helps justify premium service pricing and builds homeowner trust.

### "Can it handle all of our properties?"

The pilot starts small on purpose. We validate the workflow with a few properties first. If it works, the system can expand to more properties and team members.

### "We need custom features."

That is normal. The pilot helps identify which custom needs are truly important. Early customers may help shape the roadmap, but the first goal is to validate the core workflow.

### "How secure is it?"

Security is a major production priority. EstateIQ is designed to use private storage, authenticated access, and property-level permissions as it moves into production SaaS.

For pilots, we should avoid entering highly sensitive data until the production authentication and authorization layers are fully in place.

- customer wants more properties added
- customer is willing to pay after pilot
- customer gives clear product feedback

## Recommended Pilot Pricing Strategy

The goal is to remove friction while still establishing that EstateIQ has value.

Do not make the first pilots free forever.

Recommended approach:

### Option 1: Free Structured Pilot

Best for first 1-2 customers.

Terms:

- 30 days
- 3-5 properties
- weekly feedback call
- permission to use anonymized feedback
- conversion discussion at end

Use when:

- customer is highly strategic
- customer gives strong feedback
- customer could become a reference

Risk:

- free users may not take it seriously

Long-term pricing should likely be per property per month.

Possible future pricing:


Possible per-property pricing:


The right price depends on whether EstateIQ is sold as:

- software
